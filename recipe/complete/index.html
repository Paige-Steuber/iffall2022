A to Z Bread

Prep: 1 hour 20 minutes

A fruit bread recipe where you can use any fruit!

Source: food.com

Serve warm with some butter.

Makes 2 large loaves

"An extremely versatile bread. Very moist and delicious. It's a great way to use up odd and ends of frozen berries."

Ingredients

  • 3 c. flour
  • 1 t. salt
  • 1 t. baking soda
  • 2 t. cinnamon
  • 1/2 t. nutmeg
  • 1/2 t. baking powder
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 c. oil
  • 2 c. sugar
  • 2 c. A to Z*
  • 3 t. vanilla
  • 1 c. nuts, chopped

*A-Z

-use one of the following, equal to 2 cups except as indicated:

  • Apples, grated
  • Applesauce
  • Apricots, chopped
  • Bananas, mashed
  • Carrots, grate
  • Cherries, pitted and chopped
  • Coconut (fresh), grated
  • Dates, pitted and finely chopped
  • Eggplant, ground up
  • Figs, finely chopped
  • Grapes (seedless)
  • Honey (omit sugar above)
  • Lemons (use only ½ c. juice)
  • Marmalade (omit 1 c. sugar)
  • Oranges, chopped
  • Peaches, chopped
  • Pepperminut (use only ½ c.)
  • Pear, chopped
  • Pineapple (crushed)
  • Prunes, chopped (use only 1 c.)
  • Pumpkins (canned)
  • Raisins
  • Raspberries
  • Rhubarb, finely chopped
  • Strawberries
  • Sweet potato, grated coarsely
  • Tapioca, cooked
  • Tomatoes (use only ½ c. sugar)
  • Yams, cooked and mashed
  • Yogurt (plain or flavored)
  • Zucchini, well drained

Directions

  1. Shift dry ingredients; set aside.
  2. Beat eggs in large bowl; add oil and sugar. Cream well.
  3. Add A to Z and vanilla. Add dry ingredients. Mix well. Add nuts.
  4. Spoon into 2 well-greased loaf pans. Bake in a preheated oven at 325 F for 1 hour.
